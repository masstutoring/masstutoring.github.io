College Board

Official College Board Completely Free Best Official Resource for Targeted Practice

The official library of real SAT Suite questions. You can filter by section (Math or Reading and Writing), by content domain, by specific skill, and by difficulty, then export a custom practice set with answer explanations.

Why we recommend it & details

Why we recommend it: These are real questions from the people who write the SAT, and the filters let you drill exactly the skills you missed on a practice test. It is the single best place for targeted official practice, and it is completely free with no account needed.

Question bankBoth sectionsAll levels

Know before you go: Questions come as printable sets rather than an interactive timed interface, so pair it with Bluebook for realistic timed practice. Don't churn through questions without reviewing every mistake.

Bluebook (Official Digital Testing App) (opens in a new tab)

College Board

Official College Board Completely Free Essential — Start Here

Bluebook is the app you will use on real test day. It includes full-length official adaptive practice tests, test previews, and the same built-in Desmos calculator, timer, annotation, and flagging tools you'll have on the actual SAT.

Why we recommend it & details

Why we recommend it: It is the only place to practice in the exact interface you will see on test day, with official adaptive scoring. Your first study step should be a full-length diagnostic test here, and every practice test after that should be here too.

Official toolBoth sectionsAll levels

Requires a free account.

Know before you go: There is a limited number of official full-length tests (10+ as of our last review), so don't burn through them carelessly — take one only when you can review it thoroughly afterward. Requires installing the app and signing in with a free College Board account.

The Critical Reader: The Complete Guide to SAT Reading (opens in a new tab)

Erica L. Meltzer

Paid Resource Best for Reading

A chapter-by-chapter guide to every digital SAT reading question type, with step-by-step approaches, vocabulary help, and test-style practice questions. Meltzer also publishes a separate Reading & Writing Test Book with three full-length practice section sets.

Why we recommend it & details

Why we recommend it: Meltzer's books are the most widely respected third-party materials for SAT verbal — precise, honest about what the test rewards, and updated specifically for the digital format. If Reading is your weak spot, this is the book we'd hand you.

Know before you go: Covers reading skills, not math. Buy from the author's site or a bookstore, and confirm you're getting the digital-SAT (6th) edition — older editions target the retired paper test.

Paid Resource Best Strategy Book

A strategy-first book: how SAT questions are constructed, why wrong answers are tempting, and systematic walkthroughs of official practice-test questions. It contains no practice tests of its own — it's designed to be used alongside official College Board tests.

Why we recommend it & details

Why we recommend it: The best-known book for changing how you think about the test rather than re-teaching school content. Strongest for students scoring mid-range who keep missing questions they 'should' get.

Study plans

A plan for your timeline, not a generic checklist.

One month or six, weekends-only or aiming for 1500+ — every plan runs the same proven engine: diagnose with an official test, drill your weakest skills, retest, adjust.

Pick your study plan

  1. Diagnose

    One full Bluebook practice test, real conditions.

  2. Review & target

    Categorize every miss. Pick 2–3 weak skills.

  3. Learn & drill

    Lessons, then official questions on exactly those skills.

  4. Retest & adjust

    Every 2–3 weeks. New results, new targets.
