Frequently asked SAT questions

Quick answers, with links into the guide for depth. Policies and numbers below were last reviewed July 15, 2026 — anything official should be confirmed at the College Board source.

The test itself

How is the digital SAT structured?

Two sections — Reading & Writing, then Math — each split into two adaptive modules, with a short break between sections. Your Module 1 performance determines your Module 2 difficulty, and the whole test runs a bit over two hours. Full explanation in Start Here.

How is it scored?

Each section is scored 200–800, for a 400–1600 total. There's no penalty for wrong answers, so never leave a blank. Harder second modules unlock higher score ranges.

Can I use a calculator? What's Desmos?

Yes — the entire Math section allows a calculator, and a Desmos graphing calculator is built into the testing app. Learning it well is one of the biggest digital-SAT-specific advantages. See the Desmos guide.

Studying

Where should I start?

Learn the test format, take one official practice test in Bluebook, review it thoroughly, and study your specific weaknesses. That sequence is the whole secret; the Start Here page walks through it.

Can I really prepare without paying for anything?

Yes. Official practice tests, the official Question Bank, and Khan Academy's complete course are all free — that's the core of strong preparation. Books can add value in specific cases (and libraries carry them). See books for honest guidance.

How long should I study?

For most students, two to three months of consistent work (roughly 6–8 hours a week) is a realistic runway. Shorter and longer plans exist too — see study plans.

How often should I take practice tests — and are there enough?

Every two to three weeks during active prep. Bluebook had 10+ official full-length tests at our last review; take each one seriously and review it fully. See practice.

Logistics and scores

How do I register, and what does it cost?

Register through College Board (see test day & logistics). There's a registration fee, but fee waivers (opens in a new tab) cover eligible students — ask your counselor.

How many times should I take the SAT?

There's no universal number. One to three well-prepared attempts is typical; retake when you have a concrete improvement plan, and remember many colleges superscore. Full discussion under retakes.

What are superscoring and Score Choice?

Superscoring means a college combines your best section scores across test dates. Score Choice lets you pick which test dates to send. Policies vary by college — verify on each school's admissions page. More under sending scores.

Colleges are test-optional now — should I even take it?

Often yes: a good score can still help at test-optional schools and for scholarships, and some colleges have returned to requiring scores. Take a free practice test, see where you stand, then decide. See the score-submission guide.
