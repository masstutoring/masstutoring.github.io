Recommended SAT books

You can absolutely prepare well without buying anything — the free resources in this guide cover the whole test. But a few books earn their price for specific needs. Here are the ones we'd actually recommend, and how to get them without paying full price.

Check your library first. School and public libraries usually carry current SAT books, and interlibrary loan can get almost any title. School counseling offices often have copies too. If you do buy, used copies are fine — as long as the edition targets the digital SAT. We have no affiliate relationships; we earn nothing from any book you buy.

Edition matters more than title. The SAT changed to a digital, adaptive format. Books written for the old paper SAT — often sold cheap secondhand — teach a test that no longer exists. Before using any SAT book, confirm the cover or copyright page says digital SAT.
