SAT study plans

Every plan below follows the same engine — diagnose, drill, retest — scaled to your timeline. None of them promises a specific score gain, because honest plans can't. What they promise is that you'll always know what this week is for.

The engine every plan runs on

  1. Diagnose

    One full Bluebook (opens in a new tab) practice test under real conditions.

  2. Review & target

    Categorize every miss (see how to review). Pick your 2–3 weakest skills.

  3. Learn & drill

    Lessons from the Math / R&W pages, then Question Bank sets on exactly those skills.

  4. Retest & adjust

    Another Bluebook test every 2–3 weeks. New results, new targets. Repeat.

Three months (the sweet spot) — ~6–8 hrs/week

  • Week 1: Learn the test (Start Here), diagnostic test, full review. Pick target score and register.
  • Weeks 2–4: Drill your weakest section's top two skills. One Desmos session. Start your error log.
  • Week 5: Practice test #2, full review, re-pick targets.
  • Weeks 6–8: Drill the new targets; add timed mini-sections (Question Bank sets with a timer) for pacing.
  • Week 9: Practice test #3, full review.
  • Weeks 10–11: Polish: hard-difficulty drills for strengths, fundamentals for remaining gaps, reread your whole error log.
  • Week 12: Dress-rehearsal practice test early in the week, then light review only. See final-week guidance.

Adjusting for other timelines

  • One month (~8–10 hrs/week): Compress: diagnostic in days 1–2, two drill cycles of ~10 days each, tests on days 14 and 24, dress rehearsal 4–5 days out. Fix only your top two weaknesses — breadth is the enemy on short timelines.
  • Two months (~6–8 hrs/week): The three-month plan with one drill cycle removed: tests at weeks 1, 4, 6, and 8.
  • Six months (~3–4 hrs/week): The three-month plan at half intensity. Spend the first two months on fundamentals (Khan Academy units start to finish for your weaker section) before starting test cycles. Also the best timeline for building vocabulary through reading.
  • Weekends only (~4–5 hrs/weekend): Saturday = learn + drill; Sunday = drill + review. Practice tests replace one full weekend every three weeks.

Adjusting for your starting score

  • Below 1000: Content first, strategy later. Live in Khan Academy lessons and easy/medium Question Bank sets; don't touch hard questions yet. Consider free tutoring — this range improves fastest with a person explaining fundamentals.
  • 1100–1200: Usually a mix of content gaps and careless errors. The error log is your best friend; drill medium/hard sets on your three weakest skills.
  • 1300–1400: Content is mostly there; points are hiding in pacing, hard questions, and careless mistakes. Timed hard-difficulty sets, Desmos fluency, and strategy work.
  • Aiming 1500+: Every point is in the hardest questions and flawless execution. Hard-filter Question Bank sets, review even correct answers for faster methods, and study your rare misses forensically. Strategy channels (see videos) help here.

Two weeks or less?

  • Days 14–10: One practice test + deep review. Pick the two highest-frequency weaknesses only.
  • Days 9–4: Daily 45–60 minute drill sessions on those two skills; one timed mini-section per day for pacing; one Desmos session if you haven't used it.
  • Day 3: Final practice test (dress rehearsal), light review.
  • Days 2–1: Reread your error log, prep your device and documents (test-day checklist), sleep. Cramming past this point costs more than it adds.

Retaking?

A retake without a changed plan usually reproduces the same score. Before signing up again: get your score report, identify which section and skills cost you, and run one of the plans above focused there. Two to three months between attempts gives enough time for real change. More on whether to retake.
