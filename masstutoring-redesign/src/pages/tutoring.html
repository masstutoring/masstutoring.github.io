The SAT Guide is enough for many students to prepare on their own. But if you'd benefit from a real person explaining things one-on-one, our volunteer tutors are here — completely free.

Request a Tutor Explore the Guide First

What happens after you submit: We typically match students with a tutor within one week, and you'll hear from us by email either way. If we're temporarily at capacity, we'll say so and point you to a group session or the Guide in the meantime — we won't leave you without a response.

We only use what you share to match you with the right tutor. We don't sell or share your information, and we don't ask anything about your family's finances.

Who tutoring is for

Free tutoring is a good fit if you:

  • Are struggling to study independently or stay accountable
  • Have specific questions the guide doesn't resolve
  • Are stuck on a plateau and want a second pair of eyes on your mistakes
  • Can't afford private tutoring, which commonly costs well over $100 an hour
  • Learn best when someone explains things out loud

You don't need to "qualify" — any student preparing for the SAT is welcome to apply.

What you get

  • A selected, trained student tutor. Tutors are chosen for subject mastery, the ability to explain clearly, patience, and reliability — and they complete a short training and agree to our conduct and safeguarding rules before they start. A recent strong digital-SAT score (we look for 1530+) is one factor we check, not the whole story: scoring well and teaching well aren't the same skill.
  • Help in Math, Reading & Writing, or both, matched to what you ask for.
  • Sessions built around your practice results — bring a reviewed practice test and the sessions get dramatically more useful.
  • Zero cost. No fees, no upsells, no "premium tier." Tutors volunteer their time.

Format: sessions are usually one-on-one video calls, arranged for a time you and your tutor agree on. We'll confirm the expected length when we match you.

What tutoring can't promise

We'd rather be honest than oversell:

  • No score guarantee. No honest program can promise a specific score — improvement depends on your own practice and review.
  • Not instant. Tutors are volunteers, so we match as availability allows. If none is free right away, we'll tell you and keep your request active rather than leaving you guessing.
  • Not a replacement for official materials. Tutoring works best alongside the free guide and official College Board practice — we don't recreate or reveal private College Board scoring.

How it works

  1. Fill out the request form

    Start on the Request a tutor page — it explains everything up front, then opens a short form asking for your name, contact method, grade, test date, optional scores, the subjects and skills you want help with, and your availability. We ask only what we need to match you well, and we use it only for tutoring coordination.

  2. We match you with a tutor

    We pair you based on subject and availability. Because tutors are volunteers, matching can take a little time when demand is high — you'll hear from us by email either way. See exactly what happens after you submit.

  3. Meet and work

    You and your tutor agree on meeting times (usually video calls). Come with specific questions or a reviewed practice test to get the most from each session.

While you wait: start with the Start Here guide and take a diagnostic test in Bluebook. Your first tutoring session is far more productive when your tutor can see real practice results. Prefer live group classes? Schoolhouse.world's free SAT bootcamps are a great complement.

Your safety and privacy

Mass Tutoring is run by students, and we take safeguarding seriously. A few things you should know before you request help:

  • Who sees your request: only the small volunteer coordination team that matches students with tutors. We don't sell or share your information. See the full privacy page.
  • How sessions happen: through approved video platforms and program-coordinated scheduling — not private, unmonitored channels. Our safeguarding & conduct page explains the rules tutors and students agree to.
  • If you're under 18: we encourage a parent, guardian, counselor, or teacher to know you're using the program. Some school- or library-hosted sessions may require an adult on site.
  • Reporting a concern: if anything ever makes you uncomfortable, email masstutoringea@gmail.com right away, or tell a trusted adult. We take every report seriously. Details are on the safeguarding page.

Accessibility and accommodations

If you need any accommodation to participate — scheduling flexibility, captioned video calls, materials in an alternative format, or help completing the request form itself — email us at masstutoringea@gmail.com and we'll work it out. You never need to disclose a diagnosis. See our accessibility statement.

Common questions

Is it really free? What's the catch?

Really free, no catch. Tutors are volunteers, the site has no paid tier, and we never ask for payment information. Mass Tutoring exists because SAT prep costs shouldn't decide SAT scores.

How fast will I get matched?

It depends on volunteer availability in your subject and time zone. We'll email you either way — and the guide is built so you can make real progress while you wait.

What information do you collect, and how is it used?

Only what the request form asks: contact info, grade, test date, scores, subjects, and availability. It's used solely to match you with a tutor and coordinate sessions. We don't sell or share it, and you can ask us to delete it at any time by emailing masstutoringea@gmail.com.

Do I need a tutor to do well?

No — and we'd rather be honest about that than sign you up unnecessarily. Many students prepare entirely through self-study with the free resources in the guide. Tutoring is for when you want individualized explanation or accountability.
