How to diagnose your SAT mistakes

The digital SAT doesn't give you a traditional question-by-question report, so most students never learn why they missed what they missed. This protocol fixes that — using only tools you already have for free.

We can't recreate College Board's private scoring or reveal questions they don't release. What we can do is give you a repeatable way to turn a practice test into a precise list of what to study next.

The protocol

  1. 1. Review every missed or unsure question

    Right after a Bluebook practice test, go through each question you got wrong and each one you guessed on or felt unsure about — a lucky guess is still a gap.

  2. 2. Name the skill being tested

    Be specific: not "Math," but "linear equations in two variables" or "words in context — transitions." The College Board Student Question Bank (opens in a new tab) is organized by these exact skills, which makes this easier.

  3. 3. Categorize the mistake

    Pick the real reason you missed it:

    • Content gap — you didn't know the concept or rule.
    • Reasoning — you knew the content but set the problem up wrong.
    • Pacing — you ran out of time or rushed.
    • Execution — careless arithmetic, a misread question, a Desmos-entry slip, or a bubbling error.

    This is the step most students skip, and it's the one that tells you what to actually do.

  4. 4. Write the correct reasoning in your own words

    Don't just read the explanation — write the path from question to answer as if teaching it. If you can't, it's a content or reasoning gap, not execution.

  5. 5. Pull targeted official questions

    In the Question Bank, filter to that exact skill and difficulty and do a small set — 5 to 10 questions.

  6. 6. Retest the skill

    A few days later, do a fresh set on the same skill without looking at notes.

  7. 7. Mark it mastered only after repeated success

    One good set isn't mastery. Mark a skill mastered only when you've hit it right across two separate sittings.

Do this inside My Study. The error log has fields for exactly this — date, skill, mistake type, and your own correct reasoning — and it surfaces your most-repeated weak skill so you know where to aim. It all stays on your device.

How the mistake type changes what you do

Matching the fix to the cause
If the cause was…Do this
Content gapLearn the concept (a lesson or video), then drill it in the Question Bank.
ReasoningStudy worked solutions and re-derive them yourself; practice setting up before solving.
PacingPractice timed sets; learn when to guess and move on.
ExecutionBuild checking habits — reread the question, verify units, confirm Desmos entries.

A tutor can help most when you know what you missed but can't figure out why. That's exactly what this protocol surfaces.
